Elastic vs. plastic deformation

The safe extension limit is the boundary between a spring that recovers and one that doesn't.

Safe range — elastic deformation
Past safe limit — plastic deformation
Safe extension limit
Elastic deformation means the spring returns to its original length every cycle — the metal deforms under load and fully recovers. Plastic deformation is permanent: the spring has been stretched past the point of recovery, and its new resting length is longer than it was designed to be.
